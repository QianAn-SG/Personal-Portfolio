A postgraduate student currently studying at the School of Information Science and Engineering, Shandong Normal University, with the research direction of skeleton-based action recognition.

Projects

Skeleton-based action recognition is a technology in the field of computer vision for recognizing human actions. It first uses relevant technologies to extract the coordinates of human body joint points in images or videos to form a skeleton sequence. Then, machine learning or deep learning algorithms are employed to excavate the spatio-temporal features within it, so as to recognize different action patterns. It has a wide range of applications in fields such as intelligent security, human-computer interaction, and medical rehabilitation, and can achieve functions such as abnormal behavior monitoring, natural interaction control, and rehabilitation training evaluation.
